Alex Eala, Venus Williams bow out of Bad Homburg doubles after quarterfinals loss.

Read the full article on Olympics.com

Rising Filipina star Alexandra Eala and tennis legend Venus Williams lost 6-3, 6-2 to Tereza Mihalíková and Olivia Nicholls in the women's doubles quarter-finals of the 2026 Bad Homburg Open on Thursday (25 June).

Eala, 21, and Williams, 46, teamed up for the first time at the WTA 500 grass-court tournament held in the build-up to Wimbledon.

The duo made a strong start in Bad Homburg, defeating Alexandra Osborne and Catherine Harrison in straight sets in the round of 16, but were stopped by Mihalíková and Nicholls in their second match.

Mihalíková and Nicholls controlled key break points in both sets to secure victory in an hour and 20 minutes.

Eala recently reached the semi-finals of the WTA 500 Berlin Open and won the WTA 125 Birmingham Open title earlier in June.

Venus Williams is set to reunite with Serena Williams for the women's doubles at Wimbledon.

The main draw begins on Friday (26 June), with the final scheduled for 12 July.
